About Me

Fletcher Walmsley

Hi, my name is Fletcher Walmsley.

I am currently a full time student at the University of Canterbury, were I am studying towards a BE(Hons) in the field of Mechatronics.

The field of Engineering has always been one of great interest to me. From a young age I have been interested in the way things operate and how I could make it better. In pre-high school years, this this meant trying to fin

d the most outrageous Snap Circuit creations (normally involving the trying to attach the fan module to some kind of cart) and forcing my Parents to stop and wait with me whenever a train passed though my hometown, just so that I could watch it. 

High school however was the beginning of my foray into proper engineering design and creating projects for whatever crazy idea I had recently come up-with. 

With access to an Electronics class from which I was able to raid for parts, I made wide array of projects, both within and outside of the school curriculum. Such projects included a USB powered temperature probe which logged data from its surroundings, and uploaded it to the ThingSpeak service over wifi. 

Another involved creating a small rover powered by a Raspberry Pi with a connected webcam, in which the rover could be driven and the viewed via a web browser on the same network.

Since being at University, the amount time I have devoted to such projects has reduced dramatically. This is something that I am actively remedying and this website serves to help show case my undertakings to the world. This is in the hope that the information can help others with their own projects, as the many websites, forums and YouTube videos have done so for me over the years.
